The Maldives Monetary Authority is under pressure to address a high current account deficit and ensure sufficient reserves to service debts. The compulsory foreign currency exchange rules are a part of this effort, albeit at the tourism industry’s expense. The Maldives Association of Tourism Industry (MATI) has criticized the foreign exchange regulation, suggesting a more flexible percentage-based system instead. According to MATI, many operational costs, including salaries, loans and imports, are denominated in U.S. dollars. The forced currency exchange risks leaving businesses with an excess of Rufiyaa, which they cannot use for international transactions.
